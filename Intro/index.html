# Intro

# Why I Enjoy (Creative) Writing

They all had the same faceless features: an endless stream of grammar quizzes, book reviews, character analyses... Although I went into senior year expecting yet another soulless year-long grind, I was pleasantly surprised by the independent nature of creative writing. Unlike previous English classes where we often analyzed the works of long-dead authors, this class was almost entirely focused on self-reflection -- an experience that has given me a better understanding of both myself and the way I process my thoughts.

To me, writing has given me a way to preserve my thoughts and identity; it is a collection of my worries, achievements, life events, and daily observations. Keeping a journal takes very little effort, but the reward is incomparably large. Though it may be easy to remember a song you heard on the radio yesterday, a delicious meal you just had, or a conversation that made you smile, these precious moments will be lost in a week's time. Some are perfectly fine with letting the past fade away naturally, but remembering these small moments (no matter how insignificant they may seem!) is very important to me.

Writing also lets me peek into my past -- it is only through comparing my current self to my past self that I can see how I've grown. This ability has also given me a greater insight into recurring themes in my life. When I wrote my Common App personal statement, I thought that that was the first time I had generated the idea "photography helps me preserve memories". While I was browsing my older notes, I discovered I had written the same prompt almost word for word nine months earlier. I believed I had come up with something new, but I was merely uncovering a forgotten thought.

Whether it was through mind-maps, poetry, visual collages, or just flat-out abstract art (Why is Mr. Weinstein not an art teacher?), Creative Writing has been all about reflection. Working on the various projects has helped me remember (Ha!) why I enjoyed writing in the first place and has given me a new way to express myself. Though it only lasted for half a year, I have a feeling I'll remember this class for a long time.






On a very unrelated note, this class has taught me a very large number of unfortunate but very helpful tidbits about myself! I've learned that I am absolutely terrible with deadlines. Dreadful, even. I really wish we had more time with the projects. :(
